  • By the definition of matrix addition, the sum of two matrices is a matrix of the same size.

  • We use \(A=[a_{i,j}]\) and \(B=[b_{i,j}]\text{.}\) The \(i\)-\(j\) entry of \(A+B\) is \(a_{i,j}+b_{i,j}\) while the \(i\)-\(j\) entry of \(B+A\) is \(b_{i,j}+a_{i,j}\text{.}\) Hence \(A+B=B+A\) means \(a_{i,j}+b_{i,j}=b_{i,j}+a_{i,j}\) for each possible \(i\) and \(j\text{.}\) We know this latter equation is valid since it uses the known commutative property of real numbers.   • The \(i\)-\(j\) entries of \((A+B)+C\) and \(A+(B+C)\) must be equal. This says \((a_{i,j}+b_{i,j})+c_{i,j}=a_{i,j}+(b_{i,j}+c_{i,j})\) for all possible \(i\) and \(j\text{,}\) and this equation is valid by the distributive property of real numbers.
