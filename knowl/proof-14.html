Proof

Suppose that \(A\) is \(m\times n\text{.}\) Then, since \(AB\) and \(BA\) are defined, the conformability ensures that \(B\) is \(n\times m\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} \mathrm{tr}(AB) \amp = \sum_{i=1}^m(AB)_{i,i} \\ \amp = \sum_{i=1}^m \sum_{j=1}^n a_{i,j}b_{j,i}\\ \amp = \sum_{i=1}^m \sum_{j=1}^n b_{j,i}a_{i,j}\\ \amp = \sum_{j=1}^n\sum_{i=1}^m b_{j,i}a_{i,j}\\ \amp = \sum_{j=1}^n (BA)_{j,j}\\ \amp = \mathrm{tr}(BA) \end{align*}
