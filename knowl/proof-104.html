Proof

Using Theorem 5.5.11, \(D^r=P^{-1}A^rP\text{,}\) from which follows \(A^r=PD^rP^{-1}=P\diag(\lambda_1^r,\lambda_2^r,\ldots,\lambda_n^r)P^{-1}\text{.}\) From Theorem 5.5.1 and Theorem 5.5.3, \(\lambda_1,\lambda_2,\ldots,\lambda_n\) are the eigenvalues of \(D\) and hence also of \(A\text{.}\)
