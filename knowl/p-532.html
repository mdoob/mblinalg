Let \(A\) be a square matrix of order \(n\text{.}\) Then

  • If \(n=1\text{,}\) and \(A=[a_{1,1}]\) then \(\det(A)=a_{1,1}.\)

  • If \(n=2\) and \(A=\begin{bmatrix}a_{1,1}\amp a_{1,2}\\a_{2,1}\amp a_{2,2}\end{bmatrix}\text{,}\) then \(\det(A)=a_{1,1}a_{2,2}-a_{1,2}a_{2,1}.\)

  • If \(n=3\) and \(A=\begin{bmatrix}a_{1,1}\amp a_{1,2}\amp a_{1,3}\\ a_{2,1}\amp a_{2,2}\amp a_{2,3}\\ a_{3,1}\amp a_{3,2}\amp a_{3,3}\end{bmatrix}\text{,}\) then \(\det(A)=a_{1,1}a_{2,2}a_{3,3}+a_{1,2}a_{2,3}a_{3,1}+a_{1,3}a_{2,1}a_{3,2} -a_{1,1}a_{2,3}a_{3,2} -a_{1,2}a_{2,1}a_{3,3} -a_{1,3}a_{2,2}a_{3,1} .\)
