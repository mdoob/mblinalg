Example of this method: Let the three points be \((1,1,3)\text{,}\) \((1,0,2)\) and \((2,1,1)\text{.}\) Let \((x_0,y_0,z_0)=(1,1,3)\) so that the equation of the plane is

\begin{equation*} a(x-1)+b(y-1)+c(z-3)=0\text{.} \end{equation*}

Using the other two points we get

\begin{align*} -b-c \amp=0\\ a-2c \amp=0 \end{align*}

The augmented matrix of these equations is

\begin{equation*} \left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 0\amp-1\amp-1\amp0\\ 1\amp0\amp-2\amp0 \end{array} \right] \end{equation*}

whose reduced row echelon form is

\begin{equation*} \left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 1\amp0\amp-2\amp0\\ 0\amp1\amp1\amp0 \end{array} \right] \end{equation*}

which implies \(a=2c\) and \(b=-c\text{.}\) Hence

\begin{equation*} 2c(x-1)-c(y-1)+c(z-3)=0 \end{equation*}

and so

\begin{equation*} 2(x-1)-(y-1)+(z-3)=0 \end{equation*}
