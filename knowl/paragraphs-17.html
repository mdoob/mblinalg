Parallel lines.

We start with two parallel lines with equations

  • \(L_1\) with equation \((x,y,z)=\vec{X_1} + t_1\vec{n_1}\)

  • \(L_2\) with equation \((x,y,z)=\vec{X_2} + t_2\vec{n_2}\)

The distance \(d\) between the two lines satisfies \(d=\|\vec X_2-\vec X_1 \|\sin(\theta)\text{.}\) Recall from TheoremÂ 4.5.5 that the length of \(\vec x\times \vec y\) is \(\|\vec x\| \|\vec y\| \sin(\theta)\text{.}\) Hence

\begin{equation*} d =\|\vec X_2-\vec X_1\| \frac {\|(\vec X_2-\vec X_1) \times t\vec n_1\|} {\|\vec X_2-\vec X_1\| \|t \vec n_1\|} = \frac {\|(\vec X_2-\vec X_1) \times t\vec n_1\|} {\|t \vec n_1\|} = \frac {\|(\vec X_2-\vec X_1) \times \vec n_1\|} {\|\vec n_1\|} \end{equation*}
