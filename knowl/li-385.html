Item 8.

Let \(A\) be an \(n\times m\) matrix (note carefully: \(n\) rows and \(m\) columns). Define \(L\colon\R^m\to\R^n\) in the following way: If \(\vec x\) is in \(\R^m\text{,}\) let \(\vec z=A\vec x\text{.}\) Note that \(\vec z\) is in \(\R^n\text{.}\) We then let \(L(\vec x)=\vec z\text{.}\) In other words \(L(\vec x)=A\vec x\text{.}\) It follows that

\begin{equation*} L(\vec x+\vec y) =A(\vec x+\vec y) =A\vec x+A\vec y=L(\vec x)+L(\vec y)\\ L(r\vec x)=A(r\vec x)=rA\vec x=rL(\vec x) \end{equation*}

(There is actually a slight abuse of notation here: \(\vec x\) and \(\vec y\) are (vertical) column vectors for the matrix multiplication, but are written as (horizontal) vectors in \(\R^m\) and \(\R^n\text{.}\) The context of the equation will always make the proper interpretation clear.)
