We start with a real number \(s\text{.}\) For a point \(\vec x \not=\vec0\text{,}\) let \(L(\vec x)=s\vec x\text{.}\) Then \(L(\vec x)\) is on joining \(\vex x\) and \(\vec 0\text{.}\) The distance from \(L(\vec x)\) to \(\vec 0\) has been stretched by a factor of \(s\text{.}\)
