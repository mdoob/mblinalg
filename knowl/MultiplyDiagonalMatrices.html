Proposition 2.8.5. Multiplication of diagonal matrices.

If

\begin{equation*} D=\mathrm{diag}(d_1,d_2,\ldots,d_n) \end{equation*}

and

\begin{equation*} E=\mathrm{diag}(e_1,e_2,\ldots,e_n) \end{equation*}

then it is easy to verify that

\begin{equation*} DE=\mathrm{diag}(d_1e_1,d_2e_2,\ldots,d_ne_n) \text{.} \end{equation*}
