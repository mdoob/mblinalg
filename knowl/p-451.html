  • \(n=0\text{:}\) For any matrix \(A\text{,}\)

    \begin{gather*} A^mA^0=A^{m+0}=A^m\\ A^0A^m=A^{0+m}=A^m\text{,} \end{gather*}
    and so it must be that \(A^0=I\text{.}\)

  • \(m=1, n=-1\text{:}\)

    \begin{gather*} A^1 A^{-1}=A^{1+(-1)}=A^0=I\\ A^{-1} A^1=A^{-1+1}=A^0=I \end{gather*}
    so \(A^{-1}\) is the inverse of \(A\) (and hence the notation).

  • \(n=-m\text{:}\)

    \begin{gather*} A^m A^{-m}=A^{m+(-m)}=A^0=I\\ A^{-m} A^m=A^{-m+m}=A^0=I \end{gather*}
    so \(A^{-m}\) is the inverse of \(A^m.\) Notationally this says \(A^{-m}=(A^m)^{-1}\text{.}\)
