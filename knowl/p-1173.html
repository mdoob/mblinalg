Let \(\lambda_1,\ldots,\lambda_n\) be the \(n\) distinct eigenvalues with corresponding eigenvectors \(\vec x_1,\ldots,\vec x_n\text{.}\) From Proposition 5.5.9 the eigenvectors \(\vec x_1,\ldots,\vec x_n\) are linearly independent. Then Proposition 5.5.7 may be used to construct the matrix \(P\) that diagonalizes \(A\text{.}\)
