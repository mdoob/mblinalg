The points \(A=(\cos(\alpha),\sin(\alpha))\text{,}\) \(B=\cos(\alpha+\beta),\sin(\alpha+\beta))\) and \((0,1)\) all lie on a circle with centre at the origin and radius \(1\text{.}\) These three points are joined by a line to the origin \(\vec 0\text{.}\) Drop perpediculars from B to the line joining \(\vec0\) and \((1,0)\) and also to the line joining and \(\vec0\) and \(A\text{.}\) The points \(F\) and \(E\) are where the perpendiculars meet the respective lines. The line joining \(B\) and \(E\) is extended until it meets the line joining \(\vec 0\) and \((1,0)\) at \(D=(d,0)\text{.}\)
