A transformation is linear if it satisfies the following two properties:

  • \(\displaystyle L(\vec u+\vec v)=L(\vec u)+L(\vec v)\)

  • \(\displaystyle L(r\vec u)=rL(\vec u)\)

Notice that \(\vec u\) and \(\vec v\) are in \(\R^m\) while \(L(\vec u)\) and \(L(\vec v)\) are in \(\R^n\text{.}\)
