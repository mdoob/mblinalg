Notice that TheoremÂ 2.10.2 can be applied to a sequence of elementary row operations. If, for example, we have three elementary row operations whose corresponding elementary matrices are \(E_1\text{,}\) \(E_2\) and \(E_3\text{,}\) and we apply them in sequence to \(A\text{,}\) then the resulting matrix is the product \(E_3(E_2(E_1(A)))\text{,}\) or, more simply, \(E_3E_2E_1A\text{.}\) Note that we are applying \(E_1\) first followed by \(E_2\) and finally \(E_3\text{.}\)
