Suppose a matrix has two non-zero entries \(a_{i,k}\) and \(a_{j,k}\text{.}\) First, do the elementary row operation \(R_i\gets \frac 1{a_{i,k}}R_i\) to change \(a_{i,k}\) to \(1\text{.}\) Then, do the elementary row operation \(R_j\gets R_j-a_{j,k}R_i\text{.}\) The matrix now has a new value in the \(j\)-\(k\) position which is \(a_{j,k}-a_{j,k}=0\text{,}\) and we have accomplished our goal using two elementary operations. Now we note that these two operations can be carried out with the single operation \(R_j\gets R_j-\frac {a_{j,k}}{a_{i,k}}R_i \text{.}\)
