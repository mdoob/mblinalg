Proposition 4.2.3. First properties of the dot product.

Let \(\vec x\text{,}\) \(\vec y\) and \(\vec u\) be vectors in \(\R^n\text{,}\) and let \(r\) be a scalar. Then

  1. \(\displaystyle \vec x \cdot \vec y = \vec y\cdot \vec x\)

  2. \(\displaystyle \vec u\cdot(\vec x+\vec y) = \vec u\cdot \vec x+\vec u\cdot \vec y\)

  3. \(\displaystyle (\vec x+\vec y)\cdot\vec u = \vec x\cdot \vec u + \vec y\cdot \vec u\)

  4. \(\displaystyle r(\vec x\cdot\vec y)= (r\vec x)\cdot \vec y = \vec x\cdot (r\vec y)\)

  5. \(\vec x\cdot \vec x\ge 0\) with equality if and only if \(\vec x=\vec 0\)
