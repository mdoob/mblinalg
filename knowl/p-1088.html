Let \(\vec v\) be a vector in \(\R^n\text{,}\) and let \(B=\{\vec x_1,\vec x_2,\ldots,\vec x_n\}\) be a basis. From TheoremÂ 4.9.16, there is exactly one choice of \(r_1,r_2,\ldots,r_n\) so that \(\vec v=r_1\vec x_1+r_2\vec x_2+\cdots+r_n\vec x_n\text{.}\) The coordinates of \(\vec v\) with respect to the basis \(B\) is then \((r_1,r_2,\ldots,r_n)\text{.}\) When necessary to emphasize the basis, the notation \((r_1,r_2,\ldots,r_n)_B\) is used.
