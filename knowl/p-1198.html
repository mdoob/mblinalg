Consider points \((x,y)\) in the plane. A transformation \(L\) is a rule for computing another point in the plane. For example, we could say that \(L((x,y))=(x+y,x−y)\text{.}\) Then, if we take any point, say \((1,2)\) we can compute \(L((1,2))=(1+2,1−2)=(3,−1)\text{.}\) Similarly \(L((1,1))=(2,0)\) and \(L((0,0))=(0,0)\text{.}\)
