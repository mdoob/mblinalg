Theorem 4.2.26. Properties of distance in \(\R^n\).

Let \(\vec x\text{,}\) \(\vec y\) and \(\vec z\) be vectors in \(\R^n\text{.}\) Then

  1. \(d(\vec x,\vec y) \ge 0\) with equality if and only if \(\vec x=\vec y\)

  2. \(\displaystyle d(\vec x,\vec y) =d(\vec y,\vec x)\)

  3. \(d(\vec x,\vec z) \leq d(\vec x,\vec y)+d(\vec y,\vec z)\) (the triangle inequality)
