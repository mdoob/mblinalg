Definition 4.9.1. Linear combination of vectors.

If \(\vec x_2, \vec x_2,\ldots,\vec x_n\) are vectors in \(\mathbb{R}^n\text{,}\) then, for any choice of scalars \(r_1. r_1,r_2,\dots,r_n\) the vector

\begin{equation*} r_1\vec x_1+r_2\vec x_2+\cdots+r_n\vec x_n \end{equation*}

is called a linear combination of \(\vec x_2, \vec x_2,\ldots,\vec x_n\).
