Now consider what happens as Algorithm 3.6.15 proceeds step by step. For any two \(i_r\) and \(i_s\text{,}\) say with \(r\lt s\text{,}\) the expansion on row \(s\) will add an extra factor of \((-1)\) if and only if the pair \(i_r i_s\) is an inversion. Thus as the algorithm runs, every possible inversion shows up exactly once and contributes one factor of \((-1)\text{.}\) The validity of Item 2 then follows from Theorem 3.6.19. This completes the proof of Theorem 3.6.13.
