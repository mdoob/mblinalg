Let \(A= \begin{bmatrix} 5\amp -1\amp -2\\ 1\amp 3\amp -2\\ -1\amp -1\amp 4 \end{bmatrix} \text{.}\) Then we have

\begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix} 5\amp -1\amp -2\\ 1\amp 3\amp -2\\ -1\amp -1\amp 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1\\1\\1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2\\2\\2 \end{bmatrix}=2 \begin{bmatrix} 1\\1\\1 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}

which makes \(\vec x=\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\\1\end{bmatrix}\) an eigenvector with corresponding eigenvalue \(\lambda=2\text{.}\) In addition we have

\begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix} 5\amp -1\amp -2\\ 1\amp 3\amp -2\\ -1\amp -1\amp 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1\\-1\\1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4\\-4\\4 \end{bmatrix}=4 \begin{bmatrix} 1\\-1\\1 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}

which makes makes \(\vec x=\begin{bmatrix} 1\\-1\\1 \end{bmatrix}\) an eigenvector with corresponding eigenvalue \(\lambda=4\text{.}\) Finally we have

\begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix} 5\amp -1\amp -2\\ 1\amp 3\amp -2\\ -1\amp -1\amp 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} -1\\-1\\1 \end{bmatrix}= \begin{bmatrix} -6\\-6\\6 \end{bmatrix}=6 \begin{bmatrix} -1\\-1\\1 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}

which makes makes \(\vec x=\begin{bmatrix}-1\\-1\\1\end{bmatrix}\) an eigenvector with corresponding eigenvalue \(\lambda=6\text{.}\) In this case we have now computed all of the eigenvalues of \(A\text{:}\) \(\lambda=2\text{,}\) \(\lambda=4\) and \(\lambda=6\text{.}\) Shortly we will be able to compute the eigenvalues of a matrix and will see that in fact \(A\) has no others.
