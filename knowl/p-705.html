The determinant of a matrix \(A\text{,}\) as given in Definition 3.3.9, is simply the first row expansion of \(A\text{.}\) The proof of Theorem 3.6.13 may be completed by showing that

\begin{equation} \sum_\sigma \sgn(\sigma)a_{1,\sigma(1)}a_{2,\sigma(2)}\cdots a_{n,\sigma(n)}\tag{3.6.1} \end{equation}

and the first row expansion of \(A\) are identical.
