Section 5.4 Eigenvalues of triangular matrices

The eigenvalues of a triangular matrix are particularly easy to compute.

Theorem 5.4.1. Eigenvalues of a triangular matrix.

Let \(A=[a_{i,j}]\) be a triangular matrix. Then the eigenvalues of \(A\) are the diagonal entries \(a_{1,1},a_{2,2},\ldots,a_{n,n}.\)

Proof.

The matrix \(A-\lambda I\) is also triangular and, by TheoremÂ 3.4.2, its determinant is just the product of the diagonal elements, that is,

\begin{equation*} p(\lambda)=(a_{1,1}-\lambda)(a_{2,2}-\lambda)\cdots(a_{n,n}-\lambda). \end{equation*}

The roots of this polynomial are the diagonal elements of \(A\text{.}\)
